# Characters of Representations

Let $G$ be a finite group and let $V$ be a finite-dimensional complex representation of $G$.
The **character** of $V$ is the function

\[
\chi_V(g)=\operatorname{tr}(\rho(g)).
\]

Characters are class functions, namely

\[
\chi_V(hgh^{-1})=\chi_V(g).
\]

## Basic properties

If $V$ and $W$ are representations of $G$, then

\[
\chi_{V\oplus W}=\chi_V+\chi_W.
\]

Moreover,

\[
\chi_{V\otimes W}=\chi_V\chi_W.
\]

## Remark

The point of character theory is that it turns a representation-theoretic problem into a problem about functions on conjugacy classes.
